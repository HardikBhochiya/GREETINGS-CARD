Navaratri

Navaratri is a vibrant nine-night festival celebrating the divine feminine energy. Devotees worship different forms of Goddess Durga and enjoy Garba and Dandiya dances with traditional attire and music.

Diwali

Diwali, the Festival of Lights, symbolizes the victory of light over darkness and good over evil. Homes are decorated with lamps and rangoli, and families celebrate with sweets, prayers, and fireworks.

Holi

Holi is the colorful festival of joy and unity. People smear each other with vibrant colors, dance to traditional songs, and enjoy delicious snacks like gujiyas and thandai in this lively celebration.

Christmas

Christmas celebrates the birth of Jesus Christ and is marked by joyful carols, decorated trees, exchanging gifts, and spending time with loved ones. It's a season of love, joy, and hope.

Uttarayan

Uttarayan marks the arrival of the harvest season in India and is widely celebrated with kite flying. The skies fill with colorful kites as people rejoice with traditional foods and music.

Rakshabandhan

Rakshabandhan is a special occasion that celebrates the bond between brothers and sisters. Sisters tie rakhi on their brothers’ wrists, praying for their well-being while brothers vow to protect them.

Ganesh Chaturthi

Ganesh Chaturthi is dedicated to Lord Ganesha, the remover of obstacles. Clay idols of Ganesha are worshipped at homes and public pandals before being immersed in water with great reverence.

Janmashtami

Janmashtami marks the birth of Lord Krishna. Devotees observe fasts, sing bhajans, and recreate scenes from Krishna’s life through plays and Dahi Handi celebrations with great devotion.
